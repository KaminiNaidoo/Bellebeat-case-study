### About the company

Bellabeat is a high-tech company that manufactures health-focused smart devices for women. The smart devices collect data on activity, sleep, stress, and reproductive health to empower women with knowledge about their health and habits. The products that Bellabeat provides are smart water bottles, smart watches, the Bellabeat app, membership, and Leaf (which includes the clip, necklace, and bracelet).

### Objective

To gain insight into how consumers use non-Bellabeat smart devices and how these insights can help guide Bellabeat’s marketing strategy to unlock new growth opportunities for the company.

Preparing the Data

The data set was uploaded by Mobius 2 years ago to Kaggle and is licensed in the CCO domain. The dataset was generated by respondents to a distributed survey on Amazon Mechanical Turk between 03.12.2016 and 05.12.2016. Thirty Fitbit users consented to the use of their personal tracker data, including minute-level output for physical activity, heart rate, and sleep monitoring. According to Kaggle, the usability of the dataset represents 100% completeness and 100% credibility. There are limitations to this data, as the sample size is small and would not reflect the usage of Fitbit users worldwide. The survey is also not recent, and the data collected is from one source, i.e., a survey on Amazon.

### Data cleaning & Transformation

-- Create a temp table called cleandata
-- Remove duplicates
-- Combine the two activity tables to create one master table
-- Create a new column called total_min by using addition operator to add the activity min
```sql
with cleandata as (select distinct ActivityDate, TotalSteps, LightlyActiveMinutes, FairlyActiveMinutes, VeryActiveMinutes, SedentaryMinutes, Calories
FROM `case-study-bellabeat-490020.activity.activity1` 
union all  
SELECT distinct ActivityDate, TotalSteps, LightlyActiveMinutes, FairlyActiveMinutes, VeryActiveMinutes, SedentaryMinutes, Calories
FROM `case-study-bellabeat-490020.activity.activity2`
order by ActivityDate)
select ActivityDate,TotalSteps,Calories,LightlyActiveMinutes, FairlyActiveMinutes, VeryActiveMinutes, SedentaryMinutes,(LightlyActiveMinutes+FairlyActiveMinutes+VeryActiveMinutes+SedentaryMinutes)as total_min
from cleandata
order by ActivityDate;
```
### Analysis & Visualizations
--Key insights

. Positive but inconsistent relationship (Steps vs Calories)

More steps generally increase calories burned
Wide variation suggests lack of personalization
2. Highly sedentary user base

~80% of activity is sedentary
Users are not engaging in meaningful physical activity
3. Significant drop in engagement mid-month

Sharp decline in both steps and calories after ~day 12
Indicates poor user retention and motivation
4. Behavior decline, not tracking error

Steps and calories trends mirror each other
Confirms users are disengaging from activity, not just the app
### Recommendations
1. Improve app reliability (highest priority)

Fix syncing and performance issues
Ensure consistent, real-time data tracking
2. Introduce personalized insights

Tailor goals and calorie estimates based on user behavior and fitness level
Provide actionable, individualized feedback
3. Build behavior-change features

Daily goals, streaks, and habit-forming nudges
Micro-goals to keep users engaged
4. Address mid-cycle drop-off

Trigger notifications or challenges around days 10–14
Re-engage users before habits break
5. Enhance coaching capabilities

Deliver context-aware recommendations (e.g., low activity → suggest short walk)
Move from passive tracking to active guidance
6. Add social and motivational features

Group challenges, leaderboards, or accountability systems
Increase engagement through community
